North and Central Qld urged to prepare for heavy rainfall

Posted
Friday, 13 April 2014  Issued: 12pm

Communities located between Cardwell and Yeppoon are being urged to prepare for heavy rainfall and flash flooding as Tropical Cyclone Ita continues to move south over North Queensland.

At 11am, the Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) advised that Tropical Cyclone Ita, a Category 1 system, was estimated to be 40 kilometres east of Townsville and 225 kilometres west northwest of Hamilton Island, moving southeast at 23 kilometres per hour.

During the next 24 hours, Tropical Cyclone Ita is expected to maintain tropical cyclone intensity and move southeast, most likely move close to or just offshore of the southern tropical and central coasts.

Safety first in the wake of Tropical Cyclone Ita

Posted
Friday, 11 April 2014  Issued: 10.20pm

Communities located between Coen to Innisfail including Cooktown, Port Douglas and Cairns, and extending inland to areas including Kalinga, Palmerville, Mareeba and Chillagoe are urged to put their safety first in the wake of Tropical Cyclone Ita.

The Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) advised that as at 9.11pm Tropical Cyclone Ita was crossing the coast near Cape Flattery as Category 4 Severe Tropical Cyclone with very destructive winds to 230 kilometres per hour near the core and gales extending out to 185 kilometres from the centre. The cyclone is expected to continue moving south south-west overnight and gradually weaken.

Coastal residents between Cape Flattery and Cape Tribulation, including Cooktown are specifically warned of the dangerous storm tide overnight and on the high tide Saturday morning. The sea is likely to rise steadily up to a level which will be significantly above the normal tide, with damaging waves, strong currents and flooding of low-lying areas extending some way inland.

Tropical Cyclone Ita to cross the coast tonight

Posted
Friday, 11 April 2014  Issued: 11.25am

Communities located between Cape Sidmouth to Innisfail, including Cooktown, Port Douglas and Cairns are urged to finalise preparations and bunker down with Tropical Cyclone Ita forecast to cross the coast later today.

At around 10am, the Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) advised Tropical Cyclone Ita was expected to make landfall between Cape Melville and Cooktown tonight as a Severe Category 5 Tropical Cyclone.

Tropical Cyclone Ita poses a significant threat to communities along the far north Queensland coast and is expected to bring very destructive winds to 300 kilometres per hour near the core and gales to 200 kilometres from the centre. Destructive winds in excess of 125 kilometres per hour may develop between Cape Melville and Cooktown this afternoon, extending to Cape Tribulation and possibly Port Douglas tonight.

Tropical Low nears the coast – far north Queenslanders urged to prepare

Posted
Saturday, 8 March 2014  Issued: 1.15pm

Communities located between Cape Keerweer to Saibai Island, including the Torres Strait Islands are being asked to finalise their preparations as a Tropical low nears the coast.

The Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) advised that as at 11.15am, the tropical low is expected to develop into a tropical cyclone on Sunday morning and may intensify further as it approaches the western Cape York Peninsula coast later on Sunday night or Monday morning.

Gales are expected to develop between Aurukun and Cape Grenville, including the Torres Strait Islands, on Sunday morning, possibly extending further south to Gilbert River Mouth during Monday.

Destructive winds of up to 130 km/hr may develop during Sunday night between Cape Keerweer and Thursday Island and very heavy rainfall, which could potentially lead to flash flooding is expected to develop over the Peninsula District during Saturday night and Sunday.

If it’s flooded, forget it – Queenslanders to prepare for severe weather this weekend

Posted
Friday, 07 March 2014   Issued: 2pm

Queensland Fire and Emergency Services (QFES) is advising northern and central Queensland residents to prepare for severe weather that could result in strong winds, heavy rainfall and localised flooding in the coming days.

The Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) has issued a cyclone watch for communities between Cairns and Mackay as it monitors a tropical low in the coral sea that could form into a cyclone over the weekend.

For flood and storm assistance contact the State Emergency Service (SES) on 132 500 and for life-threatening emergencies dial triple zero (000).  

Further information on weather warnings can be found at the BoM website at www.bom.gov.au  or by tuning into your local radio. For more information on how to prepare visit www.getready.qld.gov.au.
